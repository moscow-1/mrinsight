Flux in the Soldering Process of Electronic Products

Flux plays a crucial role in the soldering process, especially in electronics manufacturing. The most commonly used type is rosin resin flux, which is primarily composed of:

Rosin

Resin

Halide-containing activators

Additives

Organic solvents

<h2>Flux for Semiconductor Key Market Trends  :</h2>

Rise in Demand for High-Precision Electronics

With growing miniaturization in semiconductors, flux solutions that support fine-pitch soldering are increasingly in demand.

Shift Towards Low-Residue and Eco-Friendly Flux

Environmental regulations are pushing manufacturers to adopt water-soluble and low-residue fluxes, minimizing post-solder cleaning.

Increased Use in Flip Chip and BGA Packaging

Flux is critical for chip attach and ball attach processes, especially in advanced packaging techniques like Flip Chip and BGA.
