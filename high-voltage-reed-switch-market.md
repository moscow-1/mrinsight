<h2>High Voltage Reed Switch Market Overview</h2>

A High Voltage Reed Switch is a type of electromagnetic switch enclosed in a hermetically sealed glass envelope. It consists of two ferromagnetic reeds that close when subjected to a magnetic field. First developed at Bell Laboratories in the 1930s by Walter B. Elwood, reed switches have become essential components across various industries—especially in high-voltage applications.
